4,000 footers

Mount Garfield

Mount Garfield has an elevation of 4,501 feet. It is located in Franconia, New Hampshire. For a 4,000 footer it is a pretty easy hike. The last portion of the hike is steep, but the open view at the top is worth the effort.

Mount Moosilauke

Mount Moosilauke is a 4,000 footer located in Benton, New Hampshire. It is in the tenth highest mountain in the white mountain range at 4,802 feet. In 1860 there was a prospect house that was opened on the top of the mountain, but in 1942 it burned down. The stone foundation is still at the summit to this day.

Mount Osceola

Mount Osceola is located in the White Mountain National Forest. It's elevation is 4,315 feet. There was a fire look out tower at the top of the mountain but it was removed in the 1970's. There is a connector trail between Osceola and East Osceola that makes for an even better hike.

Mount Owl's Head

Owls Head is a 4,025 foot mountain in the White Mountains. It is part of the Pemigewasset Wilderness. This mountain is one of the only 4,000 footers in New Hampshire that does not have a maintained trail. However, the summit does not have the best views, but on the hike up the mountain there are some really good viewing areas.
